OUR COURSES

Acrylic Painting

Paint-Based Beginner

Acrylic painting is one of the simplest and most accessable form of painting. It is a great choice to get started in art. Anyone, regardless of age, would be able to grasp the concepts of acrylic painting with much ease.

Watercolor Painting

Paint-Based Beginner

Watercolor painting utilizes paints that can still be usable even when it has dried. Artists of this medium can use it both fresh from the tube or even the dried-version. Either way, it adds a softness to their paintings and is spontaneous when on canvas.

Sculpting

Material-Based Intermediate

Art can come in all forms, including that of sculpting. Unlike painting, the canvas is usually a clump of clay, a chunk of stone, or even some wood bark, and many more materials. Works of this medium usually last for a long time as it is easier to care for compared to a painting.

Oil Painting

Paint-Based Intermediate

Oil painting uses paints with drying oils as a binder. It is actually on the more expensive side and is a little difficult to learn, especially for beginners. However, once you have honed in your skill, you would be able to produce beautiful works of art worth the price.

FAQS

1 Is it too hard for me to learn at my age?

One of the most unique characteristics of creating art is that it can be done by anyone, regardless of color, age, economic background, etc. We believe that ANYONE can create art.

2 Do I need to purchase my own art supplies?

We do encourage you to purchase your own supplies so that you can practice at home if you so wish. The school does offer a variety of art supplies though.

3 How do I get good at art?

Practice, practice, practice. There is only so much our educators can do. They are your guides along this journey, but it is you who has to put in effort to improve on your art skills. So, the most important thing to keep in mind, is to practice!
